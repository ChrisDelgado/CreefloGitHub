Engineering

Growing up in a household with avant-garde artists as parents taught me how to live, love and create to the grave. My father, Sean Delgado, whom I miss every single day, was a director for the Directors Guild of America. He was a man with unparalleled taste in film, music, literature and fine art and greatly influenced how I have developed as an artist. He introduced me to the powerful films of Stanley Kubrick and the numerous genre eclectic albums on vinyl in my bedroom. Of those albums, Pink Floyd’s album Animals particularly resonates with me because it inspires non compos mentis ideas. The majority of my art comes from deep places in my heart. Dating back to my childhood, when I was a tiny bean in the back of my fathers old Saab 900, I would scream and cry, “ta peddy, ta peddy,” until he finally figured out I wanted to hear the Tom Petty cassette he usually played. It is memories like these that remind me why I crave such beautiful, uplifting sounds

Driven by aspiration and the experimentation of manipulating sounds to indulge ears of a diverse audience, electronic music production and the endless technical aspects around it propel me forward to create a musical zeitgeist for my generation. Although possible, it is highly unlikely for a single voice to attract the attention of a generation. Collaboration is essential in the industry. Whether the goal is to create an abstract music video or to generate contemporary visuals for a show, collaboration is crucial to executing musical innovation. In my experience, the most inventive parts of my pieces sometimes come completely by chance. I learned this through experience but also from studying the avant-garde master of composition, John Cage. He is well known for creating a chart system based on the I Ching, an ancient rolling of coins also known as “The Book of Change”. The chart system decided which mode and which of the twelve possible transpositions a composer could use at any given time. A simple controlled randomization allows the writer to explore a vast array of possible outcomes.
